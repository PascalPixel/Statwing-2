Statwing vs. Spreadsheets

Asking a simple question of your data in a spreadsheet takes minutes of shuffling data, creating charts and pivot tables, and writing formulas.

Statwing is designed solely for analyzing tables of data, so users work five to ten times faster.

If you're analyzing data in a spreadsheet and writing anything more than =AVERAGE(), you'll work much faster in Statwing.

Watch a video comparing Statwing to Excel to learn more...

Statwing vs. Statistical Software

Traditional statistical software was built for statisticians, and requires technical expertise and painstaking coding to ask even simple questions.

Statwing is built for business users and data analysts, so it chooses statistical tests automatically.

It even accounts for data issues like outliers, so you can always be confident you're running the right analysis.

Statwing translates results into plain English, so anyone can understand their data the way a statistician or data scientist would.

The statistically inclined can dive into clean, interactive output of p-values, effect sizes, confidence intervals, and more.

Watch a video comparing Statwing to SPSS to learn more...

Instant Visualization

Statwing automatically creates beautiful, interactive visualizations along with every analysis.

In spreadsheets and statistical software, creating even simple charts takes too much time and effort.

Statwing understands your data's structure, so it automatically creates exportable histograms, scatterplots, heatmaps, and bar charts.

Surveys

Understand the results of your survey in minutes instead of hours

Analyze and visualize answers to questions like "Is there a correlation between my customers' age and their satisfaction?" or "What if I filter that analysis by gender?"

Use Statwing when you want to go beyond the basic reporting tools of survey providers but you don't want to waste time learning expert statistical tools or shuffling data around in Excel.

Then share a link with clients or colleagues so they can see your analyses and run more of their own.

Academic Research

Statwing makes sure you're using the correct statistical tests, protecting your analyses against common mistakes

Statwing automatically detects outliers or too-small sample sizes, then alerts you to the issue.

Unlike any other statistical software, Statwing automatically reports effect sizes, not just p-values.

Quantified Self

Statistically analyze your quantified self data to understand if the trends and patterns you see are actually real
